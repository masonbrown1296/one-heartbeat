Amy Morin's powerful framework gives us a clear blueprint for building mental strength. Here are the 13 things mentally tough people refuse to do:

## 1. They Don't Waste Time Feeling Sorry for Themselves

Mentally strong individuals don't dwell on self-pity. Instead, they focus on solutions and taking positive action.

## 2. They Don't Give Away Their Power

They don't allow others to control their emotions or dictate their actions. They take responsibility for their own thoughts and feelings.

## 3. They Don't Shy Away from Change

Instead of fearing change, they embrace it as an opportunity for growth and development.

## 4. They Don't Focus on Things They Can't Control

They recognize that there are things beyond their control and focus their energy on what they can influence.

## 5. They Don't Worry About Pleasing Everyone

Mentally strong people understand that it's impossible to please everyone and prioritize their own values and goals.

## 6. They Don't Fear Taking Calculated Risks

They understand that taking risks is necessary for growth and success, but they do so after careful consideration and planning.

## 7. They Don't Dwell on the Past

Instead of ruminating on past mistakes or missed opportunities, they learn from them and focus on the present and future.

## 8. They Don't Make the Same Mistakes Over and Over

They learn from their mistakes and use them as opportunities for growth and improvement.

## 9. They Don't Resent Other People's Success

Mentally strong individuals celebrate the success of others and see it as inspiration rather than a threat.

## 10. They Don't Give Up After the First Failure

Instead of being discouraged by failure, they persevere and see it as a stepping stone to success.

## 11. They Don't Fear Alone Time

They value solitude and use it as an opportunity for self-reflection, growth, and creativity.

## 12. They Don't Feel the World Owes Them Anything

They understand that they are responsible for their own success and happiness and don't expect handouts.

## 13. They Don't Expect Immediate Results

Mentally strong individuals understand that true success takes time, effort, and patience, and they are willing to put in the work.

---

These principles help individuals cultivate resilience, self-discipline, and emotional intelligence, ultimately leading to greater mental strength and well-being.
