> "Warriors do not complain; they adapt themselves to the sum of circumstances which they are placed." – Bruce Lee

As a warrior, one must refrain from complaining. Complaining depletes energy and dampens team morale. Lou Holtz famously said, "80% of people don't care about your problems, while the rest are happy you have them."

## The Contrast I've Seen

During my time as an assistant women's basketball coach at the University of Illinois, I was amazed at all the complaining I heard. Despite the luxurious amenities of charter flights, gourmet restaurants, and top-notch hotels, many of the coaches and athletes still found reasons to complain. This was a stark contrast to my previous experiences as a coach at smaller colleges, where the focus was simply on making do with what resources were available.

On the other hand, during my time as the top assistant at Presbyterian College, I never heard my boss, Dustin Kerns, complain. Despite facing challenges and limitations as the smallest Division 1 school in the country, he would always push us to find solutions and "figure it out." Complaining does nothing but drain energy and positivity. True warriors do not engage in it.

## Two Athletes Who Inspired Me

In warrior culture clinics, I have met some incredible people with amazing stories. I have met two exceptional athletes from two different states, both of whom were born with only one arm. One of them was a young lady from Alabama who played basketball and softball in high school, while the other was a college lacrosse player at Belmont-Abbey College. The incredible courage they exhibit is truly awe-inspiring, and neither of them harbors self-pity.

The young lady, now 19, runs a highly successful clothing business that she started, while the young man plays a vital role on his very successful college lacrosse team. The respect and admiration that their teammates have for them is difficult to put into words and it serves as an inspiration to everyone around them. Despite the challenges they face, they choose not to complain, which is a testament to their strength of character. It was an honor to meet them both and hear their stories.

## The Hidden Cost of Negative Talk

Overall, avoiding complaining and focusing on personal growth can lead to increased resilience, improved problem-solving skills, greater self-awareness, improved relationships, and increased productivity.

Please be aware that speaking negatively about a teammate is a type of complaint. Whenever I hear someone speaking negatively about another person, I am always left wondering what they might be saying about me behind my own back. Talking badly about your teammates can have several harmful effects. First, it can create a toxic team culture where trust and camaraderie are diminished. Secondly, it can damage relationships, leading to a lack of cooperation. Thirdly, it can cause negative feelings and hurt among those who are talked about.

## Three Practical Ways to Apply This

1. **Focus on solutions, not problems.** Instead of complaining about a problem, shift to finding a solution. This proactive approach leads to better results.

2. **Try a "complaint fast."** Commit to not complaining for a set period of time. Train yourself to focus on solutions instead of problems.

3. **Surround yourself with positive people.** You are the average of the five people you spend the most time with. Spend time with people who inspire and motivate you and avoid those who bring you down.
